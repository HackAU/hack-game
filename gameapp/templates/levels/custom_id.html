Custom Ids

Alice and Bob are helping in the organization of HackUPC. They are handling the registration of incoming Hackers. After a long day dealing with lots of different ids, they wanted to come up with their own improved version of Spain's identification system.

National ids in Spain have a 8 digit number followed by a letter. The letter is determined by the remainder of dividing the number by 23. The list of letters are `T R W A G M Y F P D X B N J Z S Q V H L C K E`, where T is the letter assigned with remainder 0, R with remainder 1 and so on, up to E with remainder 22.

Alice and Bob have four requirements for the new system:

  1. Letters will be assigned in the same way as in Spain national ids.
  2. Numbers will have 18 digits, so it can include more people. Note that leading zeroes are allowed.
  3. Ids with letters 'H', 'A', 'C' or 'K' are invalid.
  4. The total number of different digits in the id should be a prime number.

After this, they wonder if there are enough ids, so they ask you to count the number of different ids that satisfy all requirements.
